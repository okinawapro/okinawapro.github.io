✅ Lessons Learned:

This activity demonstrates that data alone does not create decisions. Raw ingredient prices (Data) must first be organized and analyzed to calculate profits (Information). Only after this information is interpreted—by applying a business rule or management threshold—can a meaningful decision be made (Knowledge).

Notice that changes in data do not automatically change actions. A deliberate step is required to interpret information and derive knowledge. This mirrors how real organizations operate: systems support decision-making, but people (or defined rules) are ultimately responsible for turning information into action.
