📘 Lessons Learned

This activity highlights that information security is not only about stopping attackers, but about designing systems that ensure data is protected, accurate, and available at the moment it is needed.
Many security failures arise from everyday decisions such as excessive permissions, weak change controls, or lack of redundancy rather than dramatic technical attacks. By examining realistic situations, we see how people, processes, and technology must work together to support trustworthy and resilient information systems.
