🐑 Protect the Sheep

Move: Arrow Keys / WASD · Shoot: Space

📘 Lessons Learned

Assets: 🐑 Sheep

Threats: 🐺 Adaptive wolves

Vulnerability: Hole in the fence

Controls: Fence (passive), Shepherd (active)

This game demonstrates that security is not about eliminating all threats, but about understanding how threats behave and how weaknesses shape risk. The sheep represented valuable assets, while the wolves represented persistent threats that did not disappear when blocked. Instead, the attackers adapted by changing direction, returning from new angles, and escalating their attacks over time. The fence acted as a control that reduced risk but did not eliminate it, especially where a vulnerability—the hole in the fence—created a predictable path for exploitation. As the game progressed, the environment became more difficult and realistic, mirroring real‑world security situations where attackers continuously modify their techniques and defenders must manage risk under pressure. Effective security depends on identifying critical assets, recognizing vulnerabilities, applying layered controls, and responding quickly, knowing that even a single missed weakness can result in failure.
