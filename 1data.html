🧠 Knowledge Pyramid Sort

Objective: To reinforce the conceptual hierarchy: Data ➡️ Information ➡️ Knowledge. Drag each scenario card into the appropriate tier.

*(Professor's Note: Remember the flow! Data is just raw facts. Information adds context (who, what, where). Knowledge is actionable insight that tells us what to do.)*

🗺️ Unallocated Scenarios (The Concepts)

1. 1,200 total transactions processed on the system today.
2. The sensor reported a temperature of 35°C.
3. The average traffic speed was 25 mph at 8 AM.
4. The raw system log recorded 'Login Successful' 45 times.
Miami's average temperature for May was 27°C.
Last month's average traffic speed was 25 mph, which is a 10% increase from the month before.
The high number of successful logins indicates increased staff presence during the night shift.
Sales were up 15% compared to the same month last year.
Rainfall rates increased by 20% compared to the historical average for this time of year.
Based on temperature and sales data, we should launch a discounted swimwear promotion on Monday.
Since traffic speed is high and rainfall is also increasing, the city should reroute public transport along Main St. starting tomorrow.
The increased activity and successful logins suggest that we need to hire at least two additional security staff members for the next quarter.

📊 DATA (Raw Facts)

Discrete, uncontextualized measurements (e.g., numbers, single readings).

📝 INFORMATION (Tells a Story)

Data + Context. Answers 'who,' 'what,' 'when,' and 'where.' Creates a narrative.

🚀 KNOWLEDGE (Informs Action)

Information + Insight. Answers 'why' and 'how.' Leads to decisions or strategies.
